About

Hi there!

Hi, I’m Manikho, a PhD student in the Department of Earth Sciences, at IIT Bombay. I am a part of the seismology lab research team, working under the guidance of Prof. Satish Maurya. With the increasing focus on cost-effective and sustainable geophysical exploration, my research is dedicated to passive seismic methods for imaging the shallow subsurface of the Indian continent.

Currently, I am working on sub-basaltic imaging using joint Bayesian transdimensional inversion with passive seismic data. Integrating data from multiple passive seismic methods enhances subsurface constraints and helps mitigate non-uniqueness, a common challenge in geophysical studies. Additionally, I am working on improving the Bayesian framework and developing a process for jointly inverting various passive seismic datasets.

Research Interests

Shallow Subsurface Imaging, Surface Wave Tomography, Waveform Modeling, Inverse Problems. .
Subduction zone structure and dynamics using seismic imaging.
